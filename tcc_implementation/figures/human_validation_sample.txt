RESPOSTA GERADA (Llama):
  According to the context, Market Cap is the share price multiplied by the number of shares, representing what people think the company is worth. On the other hand, NAV (Net Asset Value) is the total value of a company's assets minus its liabilities, also known as book value. The key difference between the two is that Market Cap reflects not only the current value of a company's assets but also investors' expectations of future earnings and growth, whereas NAV is a more static measure based on the company's current balance sheet.

In other words, Market Cap takes into account the "perceived present value of all future earnings" (as mentioned in one of the snippets), while NAV only considers the current value of assets minus liabilities. This is why Market Cap can be higher than NAV for companies with high growth potential or strong expected future earnings.

RESPOSTA DE REFERÊNCIA:
  The market cap is simply the share price multiplied by the number of shares. It doesn't indicate any inherent value; rather, it reflects what people believe the company is worth. NAV, or net asset value, is the total value of the company's assets divided by the number of shares. This is also referred to as the book value. In summary, market cap is calculated by multiplying the share price by the number of shares.

RESPOSTA GERADA (Llama):
  Enterprise Value is the total firm value, excluding cash and debt, which makes it useful for analyzing transaction multiples in mergers and acquisitions (M&A). It's the price you would be willing to pay to acquire the company, assuming it's delivered debt-free and cash-free. This means that cash on the balance sheet at the time of the transaction is not included in an acquisition, so companies with a lot of cash may have a lower Enterprise Value than Market Value.

RESPOSTA DE REFERÊNCIA:
  Enterprise value is the estimated cost of acquiring a company. Typically, companies are acquired without any debt or cash, so any cash on the balance sheet at the time of the transaction is given to the original owners. The Enterprise Value represents the fundamental total value of the firm. It excludes cash because paying for cash with cash doesn't make sense. This also simplifies the analysis of transaction multiples, as some companies might have a significant amount of extra cash on their balance sheet, which could distort the multiples.
